The story

A blank wall, a full block, and ninety feet of possibility.

Maple Street is the kind of block where the corner store knows your order and kids still chalk the sidewalk. But the wall on the corner of Maple and 4th — ninety feet of brick facing the bus stop — has been an eyesore since the hardware store closed. Every kid who waits for the 12 bus stares at grey.

Last spring, muralist Rosa Imani walked the block with us and sketched something better: a sweep of river blues and harvest golds, the old Delgado sign reborn, portraits of the elders who built this neighborhood, and a band of open space where local kids will paint their own panel during a summer workshop.

We've done the hard, unglamorous part. The building owner signed off. The city approved the design and the permits. Rosa is donating two weeks of her time. What's left is real money for real things — and that's where you come in.

"A neighborhood paints its own face. We just want ours to look like the people who live here." — Rosa Imani, lead artist
