I used to keep my notes the way most people do: a folder of files I'd never open
again, a notes app with 4,000 orphans, the occasional voice memo to myself in the
car. None of it talked to each other, so none of it grew.

A glasshouse is a place where you tend things that aren't ready for the open
ground yet. That's what this is — a slow, public collection of [[Digital gardens|notes
in public]] that I water when I have something to add and ignore when I don't. Some
are barely seedlings. A few have been here long enough that I'd defend them.

The rule I'm trying to keep: **write to think, not to publish.** That's the whole
idea behind [[Working with the garage door up]] — let people see the workshop, not
just the finished furniture. The mess is the point.

If you're new here, the honest entry points are [[Tending notes]] (how this place
actually works) and [[Digital gardens]] (why it's shaped the way it is). Everything
else you can reach by following links, or by opening the [graph](/#graph) and
poking at it.

Welcome in. Wipe your feet, mind the seedlings.
