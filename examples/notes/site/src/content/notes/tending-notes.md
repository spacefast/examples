A garden dies from neglect, not from bad seeds. The hard part of keeping a
[[Digital gardens|digital garden]] isn't writing the first note — it's coming back.
So I made the coming-back the whole method, and gave every note a visible **growth
status** so revisiting never feels like admitting the note was wrong:

- **🌱 Planted** — a seedling. A thought I've captured but barely worked. Might be
  half-wrong. Up anyway, on purpose.
- **🌿 Growing** — actively tended. I've reworked it a few times; it still moves.
- **🌲 Evergreen** — settled. Densely linked, written in my own words, something I'd
  defend. (The bar is in [[Evergreen notes]].)

Nothing has to be evergreen. Most notes never get there, and that's healthy — a
garden that's all old-growth trees has stopped growing. The statuses exist so a
seedling can sit in public without apologising, which is the same permission slip
behind [[Working with the garage door up]] — wait, wrong note, that one lives under
its own page; follow the link.

The actual habit is small: **once a week, water three notes.** Open whatever the
[graph](/#graph) or the backlinks throw at me, fix a sentence, add a link, maybe
split an over-grown one into two [[Atomic notes|atomic notes]]. Three notes is
nothing. Three notes a week for a year is a garden.
