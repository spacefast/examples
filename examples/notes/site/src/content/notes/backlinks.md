A normal hyperlink is one-directional. You link from this note to another, and the
other note has no idea. **Backlinks** close the loop: the destination keeps a list
of everything that points *at* it, so you can stand on any note and see the whole
neighbourhood that mentions it.

This sounds like a small feature and is actually the difference between a website
and a thinking tool. Forward links are *intentions* — the connections you thought
of while writing. Backlinks are *discoveries* — connections you didn't plan,
surfacing later because some other note happened to reference this one. The garden
gets smarter without you doing anything, just from accumulated cross-references.

You can see it work at the bottom of every note here, under **Linked from**. That
panel isn't hand-maintained — at build time the site walks every note, reads the
`[[wiki links]]` in each one, and inverts the whole thing into a map of who points
where. Luhmann did this by hand in his [[Zettelkasten]], threading reference numbers
between cards; the only thing software really added to the method is doing the
bookkeeping for free.

Once backlinks exist, link generously. Every link you make is a gift to two notes:
the one you're writing, and the one you'll rediscover later because of it. That
generosity is the engine under any healthy [[Digital gardens|digital garden]].
