Who we are

A lot that was empty, now feeds the block

In 2008 a handful of neighbors cleared a rubble-strewn lot on Maple Street, hauled in soil by the wheelbarrow, and built the first six beds out of salvaged lumber. Sixteen seasons later the garden holds 48 raised beds, a tool shed, a three-bin compost system, a pollinator border, and a little free pantry where extra zucchini finds a home.

We're run entirely by the people who grow here — no staff, no storefront, just a rotating steering committee and a shared key. Members tend their own plots, but the paths, the compost, the water lines, and the welcome are everyone's.

Everything we grow is organic. No synthetic sprays, no chemical feed — because we share the same soil, the same bees, and the same kids running down the rows.

Rosa Delgado Plot Coordinator · steering committee, est. 2008

Volunteer workdays

A few hands, an hour or two, the whole garden hums

Tending your own bed is the fun part. Keeping the commons going — the compost, the paths, the shared tools, the pollinator border — takes all of us. So every member pitches in about 2 hours a month (roughly 24 hours across the season). Bring kids, bring gloves, bring nothing — there's always a job.

Next drop-in workday —
  • Second Saturday, every month · 9 am–12 pm. The big drop-in day — no sign-up, just show up. Coffee and donuts on the harvest table.
  • Weeknight waterers. Take a week on the summer watering rota and it counts double.
  • Skill days. Build a bed, fix the gate, paint the shed — handy folks especially welcome.
  • Log it yourself. Jot your hours on the shed clipboard or text the coordinator. We trust you.

Garden rules

Five things that keep us growing

Short and neighborly. Break one by accident and someone will gently remind you — that's the whole system.

🌿

Grow organic, always

No synthetic pesticides, herbicides, or chemical fertilizers — ever. We share the soil, the water table, and the bees. Compost, cover crops, and patience do the work here.

🧤

Tend your plot

Keep your bed planted, weeded, and watered. A bed left bare or overgrown for three weeks may be reassigned — but we'll always reach out first. Going away? Just tell us and we'll cover you.

🔁

Share the commons

Return tools clean, coil the hoses, latch the gate behind you. Add only green scraps to the compost — no meat, no weeds gone to seed, no plastic. Leave it better than you found it.

🤝

Be a good neighbor

Harvest only from your own bed. Keep the paths clear, kids welcome with a grown-up, music low, and — sorry, pups — dogs stay outside the fence. We're all here for the same reason.

💧

Water wisely

Morning or evening, at the roots, never midday. We all share one meter, so soak deep and skip the sprinkler. Mulch holds the moisture and your back will thank you.

📋

Show up & log it

Two volunteer hours a month keeps your bed and the garden healthy. Sign the shed clipboard or text the coordinator. Miss a month? Catch up the next — life happens.

Events

Come by — you don't need a plot

Our gates open to the whole neighborhood for these. Free, family-friendly, and usually involving food.

  • Feb22

    Late-Winter Seed Swap

    Saturday · 10 am–1 pm · in the shed (heated!)

    Bring seeds you saved, leave with new ones. Tomatoes, beans, marigolds, the lot — plus hot cider and a seed-starting demo for first-timers.

  • Mar22

    Spring Kickoff & Soil Day

    Saturday · 9 am–2 pm · all hands

    The season opener: beds get turned, compost gets spread, new members meet their plots, and the kids paint the season's bed markers. Chili on the grill at noon.

  • Jun–Aug2nd

    Summer Potlucks

    Second Saturdays · 6 pm · the harvest table

    Long tables under the string lights. Everyone brings a dish (bonus points if it's from the garden). The most relaxed evening on the block, three months running.

  • Oct11

    Harvest Festival

    Saturday · 11 am–4 pm · open to all

    Our big one. A produce table, a kids' scarecrow build, live music, a bake-off, and the famous pickle contest. Proceeds top up the plot scholarship fund.

Getting in

  • Members get a gate key at orientation — the garden's open dawn to dusk, year round.
  • Visitors are always welcome during the Saturday workdays and any event. Just push the blue gate; it's unlatched when we're in.

  • Parking: free on Maple after 9 am; bike racks at the gate.
