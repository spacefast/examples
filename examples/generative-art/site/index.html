What is a flow field?

Imagine a grid where every point quietly points in some direction — a weather map for an invisible wind. Driftfield builds that wind from Perlin noise, then releases a swarm of particles that simply follow it. Each particle traces the current it falls into, and thousands of those paths, layered in translucent ink, settle into the organic, marbled line work you see. Change the seed and you change the wind; change the dials and you change how the swarm reads it.
