**Learning in public** is the habit of doing your learning out loud: posting the
notes, asking the dumb question with your name attached, publishing the draft that
still has holes in it. The phrase is shawn "swyx" wang's, and the pitch is that
openness is a magnet — put your rough understanding somewhere visible and the
corrections, the better sources, and the people who care about the same thing come
to *you*.

It cuts against a strong instinct. We're trained to publish only the finished, the
correct, the defensible. But finished work teaches the reader and nobody else,
while public learning teaches you twice: once when you struggle to phrase the thing
clearly, and again when someone replies, *"close, but actually…"* The
embarrassment is the tuition, and it's cheap.

This whole glasshouse is the practice applied to notes. Every page wears a growth
status precisely so a half-formed idea can sit in the open without pretending to be
done — see [[Tending notes]]. The lowest-friction version of all of this is just
[[Working with the garage door up]]: don't even wait until you're "learning
something" worth announcing. Leave the door open and let people watch the ordinary
work.
